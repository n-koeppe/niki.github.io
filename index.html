About Niklas Koeppe

I'm a 24-year-old German researcher pursuing a PhD in reinforcement learning and computational neuroscience at the Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST). My research focuses on continual learning, plasticity mechanisms, and energy-efficient computation, exploring biologically inspired learning mechanisms in artificial agents.


I combine rigorous theoretical modeling with hands-on implementation in custom RL frameworks, developing systems that can adapt dynamically without explicit task boundaries. My work bridges the gap between neuroscience and artificial intelligence, investigating how biological principles like dendritic computation and interneuron gating can inform more robust and efficient learning algorithms.


With a BSc in Artificial Intelligence (Cum Laude) from Radboud University, I bring expertise in machine learning, cognitive science, and computational modeling. Beyond research, I'm passionate about quantitative trading, fitness optimization, and applying systematic approaches to both work and lifestyle domains.
